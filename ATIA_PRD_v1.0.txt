1 · EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Agency Trust & Transparency Agent (ATIA) is an AI-powered reasoning system built for the JSO platform that evaluates the trustworthiness of recruitment agencies and communicates its findings honestly to every type of user — job seekers, HR consultants, platform admins, and licensing reviewers.
JSO Phase-1 delivers a platform that stores agency data, reviews, and placement records. It does not interpret that data. Job seekers today see a list of agencies with star ratings and have no way to distinguish genuinely trustworthy agencies from those with manipulated reputations. ATIA changes that.

The Agent's Core Principle
ATIA will always show users the truth — including when the truth is that an agency's reviews appear to be manipulated. An agent that hides uncomfortable data to avoid conflict with agencies is not a trust agent. It is a liability. Honesty is not a feature. It is the product.

ATIA operates across all four JSO dashboards, each receiving a purpose-built presentation of the same underlying trust intelligence. It runs in both reactive mode (on-demand evaluation when a user views an agency) and proactive mode (continuous background re-evaluation when new data arrives or anomalies emerge).
Built on Anthropic Claude Opus 4.6 and integrated into JSO's existing Next.js / Node.js / Supabase stack, ATIA is designed to be transparent, auditable, resistant to gaming, and respectful of user privacy.

2 · PROBLEM STATEMENT

2.1  The Core Problem
Job seekers on JSO cannot determine which recruitment agencies are trustworthy. They see raw star ratings and review counts but receive no interpretation, no context, and no warning when the data they are viewing has been manipulated.

Problem Dimension
Current State
Impact on Users
Information asymmetry
Agencies understand their own track record. Job seekers have no equivalent insight.
Candidates make career-affecting decisions with structurally inferior information.
Manipulation incentive
Trust signals directly affect agency visibility. There is a financial incentive to game reviews and ratings.
Manipulated agencies appear as trustworthy as genuine ones.
Interpretation gap
A 4.0 rating from 200 reviews and a 4.0 rating from 5 reviews look identical on Phase-1.
High-volume, authentic agencies are not differentiated from low-data, unverified ones.
No anomaly detection
JSO has zero existing fraud detection infrastructure.
Coordinated fake review campaigns are invisible to the platform.
Cross-signal blindness
Reviews, placement rates, and feedback scores are displayed independently.
An agency with glowing reviews and a 9% placement rate looks credible.
No audience-aware delivery
All users see the same raw data regardless of their needs.
An admin who needs anomaly flags gets the same view as a job seeker who needs a simple verdict.

2.2  Why This Cannot Be Solved Without an AI Agent
The combination of natural language review analysis, multi-signal weighting, anomaly detection, and audience-specific communication cannot be reduced to a formula or a rule-based system. Each of these sub-problems requires contextual reasoning:
Detecting manipulated reviews requires understanding natural language patterns AND statistical patterns AND timing patterns simultaneously.
Weighting signals requires understanding how volume, recency, and verification status interact — and that interaction changes with context.
Writing an honest, plain-language explanation of a trust verdict requires generating nuanced prose that is accurate, fair, and actionable.
These are reasoning tasks. They require an agent.

4 · USER PERSONAS

Persona 1 — The Job Seeker (Primary User)
Priya, 28 — Mid-level Software Engineer
Priya is actively looking for a new role. She has no personal network in the recruitment industry and relies entirely on platform signals to evaluate agencies. She is smart and motivated but has been burned before by an agency that appeared credible and delivered nothing. She does not want technical jargon — she wants a clear answer: can I trust this agency with my career?
What ATIA gives Priya: a plain-language trust card on every agency page that tells her what the evidence says, what concerns exist, and — critically — whether any suspicious activity was detected. She can make an informed decision in under 30 seconds.

Persona 2 — The HR Consultant
Marcus, 35 — Senior Recruitment Consultant at TechBridge Recruitment
Marcus manages placements for TechBridge. His reputation and income depend on client outcomes. He wants to understand how his agency is perceived on JSO, what candidates are saying, and where he can improve. He also wants to know how he compares to other agencies in his sector.
What ATIA gives Marcus: a consultant-facing trust view that shows TechBridge's profile, the signals behind it, and a benchmark comparison. He sees actionable improvement areas without being exposed to raw manipulation detection logic that would be irrelevant to him.

Persona 3 — The Super Admin
Aisha, 42 — JSO Platform Operations Lead
Aisha is responsible for platform integrity. She needs visibility into agencies flagged for anomalous behaviour, the ability to audit any trust verdict, and the authority to override an evaluation with a documented reason. She is not looking for simplified summaries — she needs full data.
What ATIA gives Aisha: a Trust Intelligence panel with complete evaluation breakdowns, all integrity flags, the agent's full reasoning chain, and a permanent audit trail. Every override she makes is logged with her name and reason.

Persona 4 — The Licensing Reviewer
James, 38 — JSO Licensing & Compliance Officer
James reviews agency applications for platform licensing and renewal. He needs a structured, evidence-based report for each agency that tells him whether they meet minimum trust thresholds and flags any compliance concerns. His decisions are formal and documented.
What ATIA gives James: an auto-generated Trust Compliance Report formatted for licensing decisions, with minimum threshold pass/fail indicators and a structured summary of any active integrity flags.

6 · AGENT BEHAVIOUR SPECIFICATION

This section defines the agent's reasoning process with the precision required for engineering implementation. Every step is described in terms of inputs, decision logic, outputs, and failure conditions.

6.1  Agent Architecture: Two Cooperating Sub-Agents
Why Two Sub-Agents?
Sub-Agent A handles data retrieval and signal integrity assessment. Sub-Agent B handles trust profile construction and explanation generation. Separating them means each can fail, be improved, and be audited independently. A data retrieval failure does not corrupt the reasoning layer. A change to the scoring logic does not require retesting the manipulation detection layer.

Sub-Agent
Role
LLM Calls
Primary Failure Mode
Sub-Agent A: Signal Auditor
Retrieve all agency data signals. Run all five integrity checks. Return a structured SignalPackage with integrity annotations.
1 LLM call (sentiment-rating mismatch check). Statistical checks are rule-based.
Missing data fields, database timeout, insufficient data volume.
Sub-Agent B: Trust Reasoner
Receive annotated SignalPackage. Apply weighting model. Synthesise trust tier and confidence. Generate all audience explanations.
1 LLM call (trust synthesis + explanation generation for all 4 audiences).
Hallucinated data references, inconsistent tier-explanation pairs, explanation too vague.

6.2  Complete Reasoning Flow
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
  ATIA EVALUATION PIPELINE — COMPLETE STEP SPECIFICATION
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

STEP 1 │ TRIGGER CLASSIFICATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Input : { agency_id, trigger_type, requestor_role, timestamp }
  Logic : Classify trigger as one of:
            ON_DEMAND     — user opened agency page
            NEW_REVIEW    — review submitted event
            PLACEMENT_UPD — placement record updated
            CRON_DAILY    — scheduled 02:00 UTC
            OVERRIDE_CHK  — admin override verification
  Cache check: if trigger = ON_DEMAND AND cached profile exists
               AND age < 24h AND no events since last eval
               → RETURN CACHED PROFILE (skip Steps 2–8)
  Output: enriched trigger object + cache_decision flag

STEP 2 │ DATA RETRIEVAL  [Sub-Agent A begins]
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Queries to Supabase (all within a single transaction window):
    Q1: SELECT * FROM reviews WHERE agency_id = ? ORDER BY created_at DESC
    Q2: SELECT * FROM placements WHERE agency_id = ?
    Q3: SELECT * FROM feedback_ratings WHERE agency_id = ?
    Q4: SELECT account_age_days FROM users WHERE id IN (reviewer_ids)
    Q5: SELECT * FROM trust_profiles WHERE agency_id = ? LIMIT 1
    Q6: SELECT registration_date, activity_log FROM agencies WHERE id = ?
  Failure handling: if any query fails → abort, return EVAL_ERROR
  Output: RawDataPackage { reviews[], placements[], ratings[],
          reviewer_metadata[], previous_profile?, agency_meta }

STEP 3 │ DATA SUFFICIENCY CHECK
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Rule: IF reviews.length < 10 AND placements.length < 5
          → SET tier = 'InsufficientData'
          → SET confidence = 'N/A'
          → SKIP Steps 4–6
          → PROCEED to Step 7 with InsufficientData profile
  Rule: IF 10 ≤ reviews.length < 25 → reduce max_confidence to 'Low'
  Rule: IF 25 ≤ reviews.length < 75 → reduce max_confidence to 'Medium'
  Rule: IF reviews.length ≥ 75 → max_confidence eligible for 'High'
  Output: sufficiency_result { is_sufficient, max_confidence }

STEP 4 │ SIGNAL INTEGRITY ASSESSMENT — FIVE CHECKS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  CHECK A — Review Velocity
    Compute: daily_avg_90d = reviews in last 90d / 90
    Compute: daily_avg_14d = reviews in last 14d / 14
    Flag IF: daily_avg_14d > (3 × daily_avg_90d) AND
             daily_avg_14d > 2  (absolute minimum, ignore micro-agencies)
    Flag severity: P0

  CHECK B — Rating Uniformity
    Compute: pct_identical = max_count(rating_value) / total_ratings_30d
    Flag IF: pct_identical ≥ 0.95 AND total_ratings_30d ≥ 10
    Flag severity: P0

  CHECK C — Reviewer Account Age
    Compute: pct_new = count(account_age_days < 30) / count(reviews_14d)
    Flag IF: pct_new ≥ 0.40 AND count(reviews_14d) ≥ 8
    Flag severity: P0

  CHECK D — Cross-Signal Consistency
    Compute: avg_sentiment_score from Claude (Step 4D LLM call)
    Compute: placement_rate = successful_placements / total_placements
    Flag IF: avg_sentiment_score > 0.70 AND placement_rate < 0.20
    Flag severity: P0

  CHECK E — Sentiment-Rating Mismatch  [LLM sub-call]
    Sample: last 20 reviews with written text
    LLM prompt: classify each review sentiment as POS/NEG/NEU
    Compute: mismatch_pct = reviews where sentiment ≠ star_polarity
    Flag IF: mismatch_pct ≥ 0.30 AND sample_size ≥ 10
    Flag severity: P1

  Output: integrity_result { flags[], any_p0_flag, any_flag }
  Rule: if any_p0_flag = true → trust_tier MUST be 'UnderReview'

STEP 5 │ SIGNAL WEIGHTING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Base weights:  reviews=0.40  placements=0.40  ratings=0.20

  Adjustments applied in sequence:
    Recency boost: reviews in last 30d weighted 1.5×
    Volume discount: if review_count < 25, reduce review weight by 20%
    Integrity penalty: each active flag reduces its signal's weight by 40%
    Verification bonus: if placement_source = 'platform_tracked'
                        placement weight increases by 25%
    Self-report penalty: if placement_source = 'self_reported'
                         placement weight decreases by 30%

  Normalise weights to sum to 1.0 after adjustments
  Output: WeightedSignalSet { signal, weight, integrity_annotation }[]

STEP 6 │ TRUST PROFILE SYNTHESIS  [Sub-Agent B — main LLM call]
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Input to LLM: WeightedSignalSet + integrity_result + sufficiency_result
                + agency_meta + previous_profile (if exists)
  LLM task: determine trust_tier (if not forced by integrity step)
            determine confidence_level
            identify top 3 key_strengths (evidence-backed)
            identify top 3 key_concerns (evidence-backed)
            write explanation (50–150 words, plain English)
            write all 4 audience_summaries
            write tier_change_note if tier differs from previous
  LLM constraint: NEVER reference a specific reviewer by name
                  NEVER invent data not present in the input
                  ALWAYS reference at least one specific signal
  Output: RawProfileDraft (validated before storage in Step 7)

STEP 7 │ VALIDATION & STORAGE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Validate: all required TrustProfile fields present
  Validate: trust_tier is one of five allowed values
  Validate: if any_p0_flag AND tier ≠ 'UnderReview' → OVERRIDE to UnderReview
  Validate: if InsufficientData → confidence = 'N/A'
  Write: trust_profiles table (upsert on agency_id)
  Write: evaluation_audit_log (append-only INSERT)
  Failure: if storage fails → return last valid cached profile with staleness warning

STEP 8 │ AUDIENCE-AWARE RESPONSE DELIVERY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Select summary field based on requestor_role:
    job_seeker     → audience_summaries.job_seeker + trust_tier + confidence
    hr_consultant  → audience_summaries.hr_consultant + benchmark_data
    admin          → full TrustProfile JSON + all integrity_flags + audit_log
    licensing      → audience_summaries.licensing + threshold_indicators
  Return formatted response to requesting API endpoint

6.3  Trust Tier Definitions (Deterministic Rules)
Trust tiers are not subjective. The following rules define each tier precisely to prevent inconsistency across evaluations:

Tier
Conditions (ALL must be met)
Override Rules
High
No active integrity flags. Confidence = High. Placement rate ≥ 40%. Average feedback ≥ 4.0. Review count ≥ 75.
Cannot be High if any P0 flag is active.
Medium
No active P0 integrity flags. Confidence ≥ Medium. Placement rate ≥ 20%. Average feedback ≥ 3.5. Review count ≥ 25.
Cannot be Medium if any P0 flag is active.
Low
No active P0 integrity flags but data quality is poor. Placement rate < 20% OR average feedback < 3.5. Sufficient data exists.
Can be Low with P1 flags active if no P0 flags.
UnderReview
Any P0 integrity flag is active. AUTOMATICALLY assigned. Agent cannot override this rule.
Forced by FR-022. Admin can override with documented reason only.
InsufficientData
Reviews < 10 AND placements < 5. Assigned before any other evaluation begins.
Cannot assign any other tier until data thresholds are met.

12.3  Quality Review Process
Because ATIA's outputs affect real hiring decisions, a quality review process runs in parallel with standard engineering monitoring:
Weeks 1–4 post-launch: Super Admin team manually reviews 10% of all evaluations weekly. Discrepancies between human judgment and agent verdict are logged and fed back to the model quality team.
Ongoing: Every admin override is reviewed in weekly product meetings. Patterns in overrides indicate systematic evaluation failures that require prompt or weighting adjustments.
Monthly: Anomaly flag false positive rate is calculated against resolved override outcomes. Target < 5% false positive rate.

16 · GLOSSARY

Term
Definition
ATIA
Agency Trust & Transparency Agent. The AI agent defined by this PRD.
Sub-Agent A / Signal Auditor
The data retrieval and integrity assessment component. Runs statistical checks. Does not synthesise verdicts.
Sub-Agent B / Trust Reasoner
The trust profile construction component. Receives the annotated signal package from Sub-Agent A and produces the final TrustProfile using Claude Opus 4.6.
TrustProfile
The primary output of ATIA. A structured object containing trust tier, confidence, explanations, integrity flags, and audience-specific summaries for one agency at one point in time.
Trust Tier
One of five values describing an agency's assessed trustworthiness: High, Medium, Low, UnderReview, InsufficientData.
Confidence Level
How much trust to place in the trust tier itself, based on data volume and integrity. High / Medium / Low / N/A.
IntegrityFlag
A structured record of a detected manipulation pattern. Contains the check ID, severity, description, evidence, and weight reduction applied.
P0 Flag
A critical integrity flag (CHECK-A through CHECK-D) that forces the trust tier to UnderReview regardless of other signals.
P1 Flag
A significant integrity flag (CHECK-E) that reduces confidence level but does not force UnderReview.
evaluation_audit_log
The immutable record of every ATIA evaluation. Append-only. Cannot be modified or deleted by any role.
Reactive Mode
The agent evaluating an agency in response to a user request or a data event.
Proactive Mode
The agent evaluating agencies on a schedule without being explicitly triggered by a user.
Cache Hit
When a valid, non-stale TrustProfile is served from storage without re-running the evaluation pipeline.
Cache Invalidation
When a new data event (review or placement update) causes the cached profile to be considered stale and a fresh evaluation is queued.
Signal Weight
The proportional importance assigned to a data signal (reviews, placements, ratings) in the final trust synthesis. Adjusted by recency, volume, integrity status, and verification source.
Override
An admin action that assigns a trust tier different from the agent's assessment. Recorded permanently in the audit log with a mandatory reason. The original assessment is preserved.
Self-Reported Placement
A placement success record entered by the agency itself. Assigned a 30% weight penalty due to potential bias.
Platform-Tracked Placement
A placement success record verified by JSO's own platform activity data. Assigned a 25% weight bonus. Strongest available signal.
